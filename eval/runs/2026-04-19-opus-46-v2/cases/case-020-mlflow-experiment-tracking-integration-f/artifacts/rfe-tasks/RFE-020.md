## Summary

Training Hub users need integrated MLflow experiment tracking so that all training metrics, hyperparameters, and run metadata from SFT, OSFT, and LoRA fine-tuning workflows are automatically logged to their MLflow server. Today, users manually track experiment details in spreadsheets and notebooks, which is error-prone, makes experiment comparison difficult, and hinders collaboration. Since MLflow is the industry standard for experiment tracking and many enterprise customers already operate MLflow infrastructure, native integration eliminates manual bookkeeping and accelerates enterprise adoption.

## Problem Statement

Users running fine-tuning workflows in Training Hub have no centralized, automated record of their training runs. This creates several pain points:

- **Manual tracking is error-prone.** Users record hyperparameters, metrics, and results in spreadsheets, leading to transcription errors and missing data.
- **Experiment comparison requires manual aggregation.** Without a structured tracking backend, comparing runs across different configurations requires manually collecting and aligning data from disparate sources.
- **Institutional knowledge is lost.** When revisiting experiments weeks later, users cannot easily recall what was tested and why, slowing iteration cycles.
- **Collaboration is friction-heavy.** Sharing results with teammates requires sending files or screenshots rather than linking to live, queryable dashboards.
- **Reproducibility suffers.** Without automatic logging of all training parameters, reproducing successful experiments is unreliable.

Users currently work around these gaps by maintaining their own tracking spreadsheets or writing custom logging scripts, both of which are brittle and do not scale across teams.

## Affected Customers

- **MLflow Enterprise customers** -- over 5,000 existing MLflow users whose organizations already have MLflow tracking infrastructure in place
- **Training Hub power users** running SFT, OSFT, and LoRA fine-tuning workflows who need standardized experiment tracking
- **Enterprise ML teams** at companies including Apex AI Labs, Ledger Analytics, and BioTech ML division who require centralized experiment management across their data science organizations

## Business Justification

- **Broad user impact.** 100% of the Training Hub user base benefits from automated experiment tracking, as every fine-tuning workflow produces metrics and parameters that need to be recorded.
- **Productivity savings.** Estimated 2-4 hours saved per data scientist per week by eliminating manual experiment bookkeeping, freeing time for higher-value model development work.
- **Enterprise readiness.** MLflow is a widely adopted open-source standard with over 10 million downloads per month. Native support signals enterprise readiness and aligns with the tooling expectations of enterprise ML teams.
- **Accelerated adoption.** Compatibility with existing MLflow infrastructure across 7,000+ MLflow Enterprise customers reduces onboarding friction and accelerates Training Hub adoption in organizations that have already standardized on MLflow.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Users can point any Training Hub fine-tuning workflow (SFT, OSFT, LoRA) at their MLflow tracking server and have all training metrics, hyperparameters, and run metadata automatically logged with zero additional boilerplate.
- [ ] Experiment tracking works correctly in multi-GPU and multi-node distributed training scenarios, with only the primary process writing to the tracking server to avoid duplicate or conflicting entries.
- [ ] The feature is fully backward compatible -- existing Training Hub code that does not use MLflow experiment tracking continues to work with no changes.
- [ ] MLflow experiment tracking coexists with other logging integrations (such as Weights & Biases, TensorBoard, and JSONL loggers) without conflicts.
- [ ] Documentation is available that guides users through enabling and using MLflow experiment tracking with Training Hub workflows.

## Success Criteria

- Users can enable MLflow tracking for any supported fine-tuning algorithm and see their experiment data appear in their MLflow server without writing any logging code.
- Distributed training runs produce clean, deduplicated experiment records in MLflow.
- Adoption of MLflow tracking among Training Hub users is measurable through usage telemetry, with a target of significant uptake within the first quarter after release.
