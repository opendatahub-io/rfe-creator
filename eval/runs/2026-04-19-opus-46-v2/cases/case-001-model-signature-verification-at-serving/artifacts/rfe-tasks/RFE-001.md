## Summary

Users who sign AI models at storage time through AI Hub and Red Hat Trusted Artifact Signer have no assurance that the model actually being served matches what they signed. Signature verification is not enforced at model serving time, leaving a critical gap in the model provenance trust chain. Admins need the ability to configure and enforce signature verification policies when models are deployed for inference, ensuring that only verified models are served.

## Problem Statement

Today, RHOAI supports model signing at storage time via the Trusted Artifact Signer integration delivered through STRAT-101. However, signatures are completely ignored when models are deployed for serving through KServe or llm-d. This means the entire signing workflow is incomplete -- users go through the effort of signing models but have zero runtime integrity assurance that the served model matches the signed artifact.

Admins have no mechanism to require signature verification before a model is served, no guidance on configuring end-to-end verification workflows, and no policy controls to enforce or warn on verification failures. The result is that model signing provides a false sense of security -- it's a write-only operation with no corresponding read-time validation.

This gap affects two common model storage patterns:
- Models stored in S3 or HuggingFace with OpenSSF Model Signature files (model.sig)
- OCI ModelCar images with Cosign signatures and/or embedded OpenSSF Model Signatures

## Affected Customers

- **Financial services institutions (FSI segment):** Require verifiable model provenance for regulatory compliance, audit trails, and governance requirements around AI model deployment.
- **Government agencies:** Mandate supply chain integrity and provenance verification for AI workloads as part of security accreditation processes.
- **Regulated industry customers:** Organizations in healthcare, defense, and critical infrastructure that require end-to-end model integrity guarantees to satisfy compliance frameworks.

## Business Justification

Model supply chain security is core to the RHOAI trust strategy. The "sign on store" capability was already delivered via STRAT-101 as a strategic investment. Without corresponding "verify on serve" capability, that investment does not deliver its intended runtime integrity value -- the trust chain is broken at the most critical point: when the model is actually used.

Customers in regulated industries are demanding end-to-end verifiable model provenance. This is a competitive differentiator in the enterprise AI platform market, where trust and governance are becoming table-stakes requirements. Upstream Sigstore ecosystem projects (ImagePolicy controller, Model Validation operator) provide building blocks that the community is coalescing around, making this the right time to close the loop.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Admins can configure signature verification policies that apply when models are deployed for serving
- [ ] Models stored in S3 or HuggingFace with OpenSSF Model Signature files (model.sig) are verified against their signatures before serving begins
- [ ] OCI ModelCar images are verified (container image signature and/or embedded OpenSSF Model Signature) before serving begins
- [ ] When signature verification fails, clear pass/fail feedback is provided to both admins and users, including actionable information about why verification failed
- [ ] Admins can choose between enforcement modes: blocking deployment entirely on verification failure, or issuing warnings while allowing deployment to proceed
- [ ] The verification capability works with both KServe and llm-d serving infrastructure

## Success Criteria

The end-to-end trust chain -- sign on store, verify on serve -- is complete. An admin can configure a policy requiring signature verification, deploy a model, and have confidence that the platform validated the model's integrity before serving it. Unsigned or tampered models are blocked (or flagged) according to the configured policy. The gap between signing and serving is fully closed, delivering on the runtime integrity promise of the STRAT-101 investment.
